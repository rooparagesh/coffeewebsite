LE BITES






Black coffee:

Black coffee is made from plain ground coffee beans that are brewed hot. It's served without added sugar, milk, or flavorings.






Espresso Coffee:

A shot of espresso is stronger than the same amount of coffee and is a thicker, more concentrated liquid with a bolder flavor.






Latte:

This classic drink is typically 1/3 espresso and 2/3 steamed milk, topped with a thin layer of foam,now it come up with endless customizations.






Cappuccino:

This espresso-based drink is similar to a latte, but the frothy top layer is thicker. The ratio is equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foam.


Macchiato:

A macchiato is a shot of espresso with steamed milk. In Italian, macchiato means "stained" "spotted," so it refers to coffee with stained with milk.


Irish Coffee:

This boozy drink is a combination of black coffee, whiskey, and sugar, topped with a dollop of whipped cream,now it come up with endless customizations.


Dalgano coffee:

Dalgona coffee is a beverage made by whipping equal parts instant coffee powder, sugar, and hot water until it becomes creamy.


Cold Brew:

It's made by slowly steeping coffee grounds over cool or room-temperature water, so it tastes smoother and less bitter than regular iced coffee.
